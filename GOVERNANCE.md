# VelocityX Governance

VelocityX is published by Coreova as a free, open-source browser extension. Maruf Ahmed Limon is credited as the original creator, and Coreova is the public maintainer and MIT license holder.

## Roles

- Maintainers guide product direction, review contributions, manage releases, and protect project quality.
- Coreova holds the public publisher, maintainer, and license-holder role for releases.
- Contributors propose issues, submit pull requests, improve documentation, test changes, and share implementation feedback.
- Users report bugs, ask questions, suggest improvements, and describe real workflow needs.

## Decision Making

VelocityX uses maintainer-led decision making. Maintainers should listen carefully, explain important decisions when useful, and prioritize long-term product quality over short-term noise.

Larger changes may need an issue or discussion that covers:

- Problem and audience.
- Proposed solution.
- Alternatives considered.
- Compatibility impact.
- Privacy, permission, and security implications.
- Testing and maintenance cost.

## Product Direction

VelocityX favors work that is:

- Useful in real video and audio workflows.
- Local-first and privacy-aware.
- Clear, accessible, and responsive.
- Dependable across Chromium-based browsers.
- Small enough to maintain without unnecessary dependencies.

Maintainers may decline changes that add fragile site hacks, unnecessary permissions, remote tracking, dark patterns, unrelated branding, or broad rewrites without strong justification.

## Repository Status

VelocityX is an active open-source project unless the README or releases state otherwise.
